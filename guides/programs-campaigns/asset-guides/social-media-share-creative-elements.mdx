# Overview

This article covers the default creative elements of your program's promotional emails . Just like email sharing, social media represents an important part of your referral program.

Each of the creative elements of your social media shares has a default appearance and copy (see the tables below). To configure any of the creatives or specific creative elements of your social media shares, contact your Client Services Manager or [submit a ticket to Extole](https://success.extole.com/requests/new).

# Brand Sharing via Facebook

Facebook brand shares consist of the following elements:

| Element Name | Description | Default |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Application Name | Provides a Facebook application to Extole with the appropriate Open Graph actions. | Extole Social Referrals. |
| Title | The title element of the Facebook message, such as "15% Off." | [Friend Reward] [Descriptive Word]. |
| Description | The descriptive message that appears inside the Facebook share. | Take [Friend Reward] off your [first/next] [Client Name] purchase. Click on the link above to get you discount. |
| Brand Image | A brand image to appear in the Facebook share. Recommended 1200 x 630 px. |  |

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why can't I customize my Facebook share message?

Facebook specifically prohibits message pre-population of any kind for a user. The user must type the entire message themselves.

From the [User Message Facebook Documentation](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/submission-process/opengraph/guidelines/action-properties/#usermessages): "Users must personally write these [share] messages. Even if users can control (edit or delete) the message content, your app cannot pre-fill user messages."

## Can I customize the Facebook/Twitter/Pinterest/Social buttons and colors?

The short answer is maybe. Any social asset should be in-line with that company's brand resource guide. It is technically possible to adjust the color to fit with your own company's brand. It is upon your own team to interpret the branding rules for the social channel(s) you wish to adjust. If you deem it's within their rules, Extole can make the creative adjustments. Note that the social channel may be subject to suspension if Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, etc. deem the adjustment to be outside of their brand usage rules.
